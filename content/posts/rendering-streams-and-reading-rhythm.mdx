## Readers feel rhythm before they measure speed

A page that reveals structure clearly can feel faster than one with slightly better raw timing but poor sequencing.

Editorial products benefit from stable placeholders, strong headings, and predictable module order.

## Streaming should support comprehension

Progressive rendering is useful when the first visible blocks already tell the reader where they are and what comes next.

Performance work and information design are the same conversation.

## Performance language can be visual

Status indicators, timing chips, and measured spacing all reinforce the feeling that the system is responsive.

That feeling matters for technical writing brands.
